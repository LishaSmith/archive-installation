Intro

As we browse the internet, our computer connects with various web servers, the process is instantaneous. It’s quite easy to forget that we are actually sending requests and receiving packets while we do this. As we share information, Websites and Services store it, to better personalise your internet experience.
But how would we feel if we were confronted with the personas that our technology has created for us?

Archive is a public installation that serves as a metaphor for our activity on the internet and how accessable it is. Asking the public to complete an interactive survey and answer the questions the computer has for them, Archive develops an algorithm of advertisements for the user as well as an entry consisting of the information it has gathered through their answers. These entries are displayed publically, creating a feeling of discomfort surrounding who can access you. Archive poses one simple question: Who are you?

How Will It Work?

The key element of the installation is ambiguity. Just like the internet, this installation has no instruction and is just there to interact with at the publics own free will. Individuals may be intrigued by the bright colours and the question: Who Are You? There is no physical reward for completing the survey, if you don’t count the entry card and generated information. It would need to be set up in spaces where the equipment won’t be damaged, but ultimately the more out of place the installment appears the better. It’s a physical manifestation of the internets backlogs. A representation of what every corporation could buy, your information.

Inspiration

Face to Facebook - Paolo Cirio, Alessandro Ludovico

This project looks at how our Facebook details and photos can be exposed and maniupulated. Bringing light to how simple identity theft can be, Face to Facebook stole one million facebook photos. They posted these photos to pseudo-dating sites where users could be matched with a potential mate. Showing how Facebook is a goldmine for your data, at your own risk, without your control. Unveiling just how fragile our virtual identities really are. Archive plays into that same field. Showing what personas our computers create for us, showing just how it’s applied and showcasing it in a public space.

Memopol 2 - Timo Toots

Memopol 2 is an installation that maps the visitors information. Presenting a reality where our information is widely interconnected and easily traced. Inserting an indentification card into the machine allows it to collect information about the visitor from international databases and the Internet. This information is then displayed on a large-scale display.
I would like Archive to capture the same element of ambiguity. Archive displays the generated information on a digital card. But Archive would then store this information that can be publicly accessed.

Visuals

Archive is a questionnaire. Prompting the user to select images and answer questions. The key element is the ambiguity of the installation, the user is intrigued and answers truthfully. The only information that the user will be asked for is their name. Everything else is interpreted through the computer, down to the fine details such as grammar, spelling and colloquialisms.

Archive provides users with an analysis of themselves based off of the information they provide. Archive then creates two previews that demonstrate how advertisements are targeted toward you.

All entry cards are stored in a database that can be accessed publicly along with the generated social media advertisements.

Although the information isn’t explicitly personal, individuals may become uncomfortable knowing that anybody can look at them. This proves the point of the installation.

Why?

Cyber Security

Social Media contains almost all of our personal information. That information can potentially be accessed by hackers. That means that all of our images, messages, security questions, mobile numbers, and addresses can be located. This is becayse days all our media and browsing are interconnected, meaning there is a long list of information available that can enable identity theft.
Link

Data Mining:

Our Data is collected and analysed in order to create more personalised internet experiences for us. This leads to privacy breaches as companies and groups buy this information in order to sell you their ideas. Google and Facebook are the most notable contributers, both having controversies surrounding their data storage and selling. A Paper titled Social Media Analytics: Data Mining applied to insurance Twitter posts, sheds light on just how easy it is to collect data and predict user behaviour. Our key words and key phrases can be kept track of and used for more effective advertisements (Mosley, 2012)

Mosley Jr, R. C. (2012). Social media analytics: Data mining applied to insurance Twitter posts. In Casualty Actuarial Society E-Forum (Vol. 2, p.1).
